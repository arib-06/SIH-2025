Red Fort

🏰 Historic Monument

A UNESCO World Heritage site and symbol of India's rich Mughal history.

The Red Fort, or Lal Qila, served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for over 200 years. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, this magnificent fortress showcases Indo-Islamic architecture at its finest.

📍 Location & Timings

📌 Netaji Subhash Marg, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi

⏰ 9:30 AM - 4:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)

🎫 Entry Fee: ₹35 for Indians, ₹500 for foreigners

UNESCO Heritage Mughal Architecture Sound & Light Show
3D View

India Gate

🏛 War Memorial

An iconic war memorial honoring 70,000 Indian soldiers who died in World War I.

Standing 42 meters tall, India Gate is the national monument of India. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it's beautifully illuminated at night and surrounded by lush lawns perfect for evening picnics.

📍 Location & Timings

📌 Rajpath, India Gate, New Delhi

⏰ Open 24 hours (Best viewed at sunset)

🎫 Entry Fee: Free

War Memorial Colonial Architecture Evening Picnics
3D View

Lotus Temple

🪷 Spiritual Architecture

A Bahá'í House of Worship renowned for its stunning lotus-shaped architecture.

The Lotus Temple is a marvel of modern architecture, shaped like a lotus flower with 27 free-standing marble clad petals. It's a place of worship open to all religions and offers peaceful meditation spaces.

📍 Location & Timings

📌 Lotus Temple Rd, Bahapur, Shambhu Dayal Bagh

⏰ 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)

🎫 Entry Fee: Free

Modern Architecture Spiritual Site Meditation
3D View

Qutub Minar

🗼 Medieval Monument

The world's tallest brick minaret and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Standing at 73 meters, Qutub Minar is a victory tower built in the early 13th century. The complex also features the famous Iron Pillar and ruins of ancient structures showcasing Indo-Islamic architecture.

📍 Location & Timings

📌 Mehrauli, New Delhi

⏰ Sunrise to Sunset

🎫 Entry Fee: ₹30 for Indians, ₹500 for foreigners

UNESCO Heritage Medieval Architecture Iron Pillar
3D View

Humayun's Tomb

🕌 Mughal Garden Tomb

The inspiration for the Taj Mahal, surrounded by beautiful Mughal gardens.

Built in 1570, Humayun's Tomb is the first garden-tomb on the Indian subcontinent. It's considered a precursor to the Taj Mahal and is surrounded by perfectly manicured Mughal gardens with water channels.

📍 Location & Timings

📌 Mathura Road, Nizamuddin East, New Delhi

⏰ 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM

🎫 Entry Fee: ₹30 for Indians, ₹500 for foreigners

UNESCO Heritage Mughal Gardens Architectural Marvel
3D View

Akshardham

🏛 Modern Temple Complex

A stunning modern temple complex showcasing Indian culture and spirituality.

Akshardham is a magnificent temple complex built entirely without steel, featuring intricate stone carvings, water shows, and exhibitions that celebrate 10,000 years of Indian heritage, culture, and spirituality.

📍 Location & Timings

📌 NH 24, Akshardham Setu, New Delhi

⏰ 9:30 AM - 6:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)

🎫 Entry Fee: Free (Exhibitions extra)

Modern Architecture Water Shows Cultural Exhibitions
