A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Fatehpur Sikri is a stunning fortified city built by Emperor Akbar, blending Mughal, Persian, and Indian architectural styles.

Constructed in the late 16th century, Fatehpur Sikri served as the Mughal capital for 14 years. Its highlights include Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, Panch Mahal, and the tomb of Salim Chishti. Today, the deserted red sandstone city tells tales of Mughal grandeur.
